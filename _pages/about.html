Chris Vargas: My Story

Where it all Began

25 years old. Born and raised in Dominican Republic, a developing country in the middle of the Caribbean. Because of this we developed a mastery in problem-solving, having to come up with unique ways to solve the most basic tasks on hand. Research, Design, Prototype, and testing play a fundamental part of our culture.

When I was eight, I used to play with legos all day. I was terrific; I used to build big towers, bridges, and cars. My mother divorced, and we began living with her best friend and her two children, one of them had a disability and needed a wheelchair.

One evening we were playing pretending the wheelchair was a sport car, which resulted in a broken wheel. The wheelchair didn’t even budge. To avoid getting us in trouble, and to help him move again, I rapidly studied the broken piece, grabbed some legos and tape, and made a quick fix. The first attempt was unsuccessful, but I changed a few times until it worked again.

After that day, I started to develop the discipline of prototyping and experimentation with the end goal of helping people.

Today, I help people through user experience design, but tomorrow, it could be anything similar or maybe totally different.

What I love to do

I love making people from different backgrounds to work together and create things; I participated and facilitated numerous online and remote design sprints workshops and got certified in Agile SaFe acquiring deep understanding of the Agile methodology.

The most powerful skill I have is the ability to learn new things quickly and teach others. I love to share and get knowledge through Sharing Sessions, Workshops, Keynotes, a or just having meaningful conversations.

The Humans that shaped my career

Emil Guareño (Sr Software Engineer @ Disney ): In just one Summer, of all the things I learned, he introduced me to the world of programming, teaching me how developers solve problems in a very creative way. After he was gone, I learned web development myself, idealizing my problem-solving skills with the understanding of technology.

Camilo Sáchez (Sr Product Designer @ Hackerone): Camilo was my manager when I was working as a UX Designer in Publicis and He challenged me and uplifted me in every way. He also encouraged me to go to RG/A's Product and Service Design Bootcamp in New York and eventually became like my big brother and mentor.

Feridoon Malekzadeh (UX Design Manager @ AT&T ): Very briefly, but influential, doon taught me the power of telling stories and mentored me through a new way of growing as a professional.
