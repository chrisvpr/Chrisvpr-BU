Chris Vargas: My Story

Where it all Began

I am 25 years old and was born and raised in the Dominican Republic, a developing country in the middle of the Caribean. Because of constraints, Dominicans are masters in problem-solving, having to come up with unique ways to solve the most basic tasks on hand. Research, Design, Prototype, and testing play a fundamental part of our culture.

When I was eight, I used to play with legos all-day. I was terrific; I used to build big towers, bridges, and cars. My mother was divorced, and we used to live with her best friend and her two children, one of them was in a wheelchair.

One evening, We were playing pretending the wheelchair was a sports car resulting in the breaking of a little piece of it, and, suddenly, the wheels were braked and stop moving. To avoid trouble, and to help him to move in his wheelchair again, I rapidly study the broken pice, grab some legos and tape, and make a quick fix, it did not work the first time, but I change it a few times until it worked again.

After that day, I started to develop the discipline of prototyping and experimentation with the end goal of helping people.

Today, I help people thought user experience design, but tomorrow, it could be anything similar or maybe totally different.

What I love to do

I love to make people from different backgrounds to work together and create things; I facilitated and participated in numerous online and remote design sprints workshops and got certified in Agile SaFe for a deep understanding of the Agile way of working.

The most powerful skill I have is the ability to learn new things very fast and to teach others. I love to share and get knowledge through Sharing Sessions, Workshops, Keynotes, a or just having meaningful conversations.

The Humans that shaped my career

Emil Guareño (Sr Software Engineer @ Disney ): In just one Summer, of all the things I learned, he introduces me to the world of programming, teaching me how developers solve problems in a very creative way. After he was gone, I learned web development myself, idealizing my problem-solving skills with the understanding of technology.

Camilo Sáchez (Sr Product Designer @ Hackerone): Camilo was my manager when I was working as a UX Designer in Publicis and He challenged me and uplifted me in every way. He also encouraged me to go to RG/A's Product and Service Design Bootcamp in New York and eventually became like my big brother and mentor.

Feridoon Malekzadeh (UX Design Manager @ AT&T ): Very Briefly but very powerful, doom teaches me the power of telling stories and mentor me through a new way of growing as a professional.
